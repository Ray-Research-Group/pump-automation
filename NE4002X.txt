THIS IS THE PUMP WE ACTUALLY HAVE. The local file NE4000x.txt is the generic
NE-4000 manual and overstates the rate/volume range. Trust the numbers here for
NE-4002X firmware behavior.

================================================================================
HARD LIMITS THAT AFFECT THE DRIVER
================================================================================

- This is a MICRO-FLUIDIC pump. Rates are in the µL/min range, not mL/min.
- Absolute maximum pumping rate: 1226 µL/min  (= 1.226 mL/min) with a B-D 60 mL syringe.
- Absolute minimum pumping rate:  14.36 nL/hr with a B-D 1 mL syringe.
- Maximum rate is DIAMETER-DEPENDENT (see table). A small syringe has a much
  lower max rate than a large one.
- The numeric field is 4 digits. Internal µL/hr rate caps at 9999 (the table's
  "Maximum Rate µL/hr" column shows 9999 wherever the true max exceeds it).
- "Volume to be Dispensed" on our unit is µL only (no working mL unit on this
  firmware) and is bounded by the same 4-digit field => max 9999 µL (~10 mL)
  per single dispense. Larger volumes require continuous mode (VOL 0) + timed
  stop, or repeated dispenses.
- Syringe inside diameter range: 0.100 to 50.00 mm.

================================================================================
MAX RATE BY DIAMETER — the numbers that matter for our 12.36 mm setup
================================================================================

Our experiment uses diameter_mm = 12.36. The closest table entries:

  Syringe                 Inside Dia (mm)   Max Rate (µL/min)
  B-D 5 mL                11.99             249.4
  Monoject 6 mL           12.7              279.8

So at ~12.36 mm the hardware max is roughly 250–280 µL/min.

CONSEQUENCE: rate = 10.76 mL/min (10760 µL/min) is ~40x over the absolute pump
max and ~43x over the limit for this diameter. rate = 1.076 mL/min (1076 µL/min)
is still ~4x over the ~250 µL/min limit for 12.36 mm. Both will be rejected /
out-of-range. A valid rate at this diameter is at most ~250 µL/min = 0.25 mL/min.
